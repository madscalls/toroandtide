Sushi on the Gulf:

The Legend of Toro & Tide

It all started when a retired deep-sea fisherman named Carl "Big Tuna" Maloney took a spontaneous trip to Japan and, after confusing a sushi-making class for a sake tasting tour, accidentally rolled what the instructor called "the best dragon roll west of Kyoto." Inspired and slightly tipsy, Carl returned to the Gulf Coast of Florida with a duffel bag full of wasabi peas and a dream.

He converted his old bait shop in Venice into a sushi restaurant, ripping out tanks of live shrimp and replacing them with bamboo accents and LED jellyfish. Locals were skeptical at first—especially when Carl insisted on naming the place Toro & Tide and serving "boat-to-board" sushi using freshly caught Gulf fish.

Against all odds, the fusion of local red snapper sashimi and Florida orange-glazed unagi rolls caught on. The turning point came when a dolphin swam up to the dock during dinner service and Carl declared it the “spirit animal of sushi.” No one really knew what that meant, but business tripled after that.
